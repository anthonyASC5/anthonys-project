Introducing "PAATH" - the student-centered budgeting app empowering Cornell undergrads to take control of their finances with clarity, customization, and confidence!


Overview:

  • Role: UI/UX Designer
  • Timeline/Duration: 1 year (2024–2025)
  • Project Type: App Development
  • Tools Used: Figma
  • Problem: Many Cornell undergraduates struggle with managing day-to-day expenses and long-term budgeting.
  • Goal: To provide accessible tools and education for budgeting rent, groceries, bills, and more – tailored to the needs of financially independent students.
  • Solution: Designed "PAATH", a financial planning app that simplifies expense tracking, income forecasting, and promotes long-term financial wellness.

PAATH helps students build smart financial habits while saving time and reducing stress. With its intuitive design and student-first approach, it allows users to confidently manage their budgets and focus on academics and extracurriculars without overworking. By integrating clarity and ease-of-use, PAATH supports a holistic vision for student financial wellness at Cornell and beyond.

Our app, KareBare, is a virtual chat box designed to improve social media literacy and promote healthy online interactions for young users.


User Research

Conducted literature review on online risks, social media impacts on youth, and government policies, identifying insights into mental health challenges, harassment, cyberbullying, and screen addiction affecting young adults.

Design Problem

  • Young users need help learning social media literacy because knowing how to navigate online interactions is crucial for their personal development, but lack a proper support system to deal with online harms such as harassment, cyberbullying, and avoiding bad actors.
    • Goal: To educate teenagers on safely navigating social media.
    • Audience: Teenagers and young adults.
    • Solution: Created 'KareBare', a virtual chatbot that educates teens on social media etiquette and safety.

KAREBARE

Coala: Our project is designed to improve communication between college students in long-distance relationships by allowing for stronger passive conveyance of emotions and providing a creative bonding outlet.


User Research

LDR couples often connect at night through virtual activities to maintain their relationship, but text-based communication can lead to misunderstandings, as it lacks emotional nuance.

Design Problem

  • College students in long-distance relationships struggle to communicate with their partners due to their busy schedules. Text, as their main mode of communication, leads to misunderstandings that make navigating long-distance relationships even more difficult.
  • Goal: Improve emotional communication and bonding between long-distance couples through creative digital means.
  • Audience: College students in long-distance relationships.
  • Solution: Developed a tool to promote passive emotional sharing and shared virtual experiences for stronger relationship connection.

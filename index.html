PAATH

Introducing PAATH: A student-centered budgeting app empowering Cornell undergrads to take control of their finances with clarity, customization, and confidence.

Our mission is to empower financially independent or semi-independent Cornell undergraduates to take control of their future by offering accessible, customizable budgeting tools and education that address their unique, immediate needs.

We aim to alleviate the stress of managing day-to-day expenses—such as rent, groceries, and bills—while fostering smart budgeting habits that pave the way for long-term financial stability. Through a student-focused solution that simplifies financial planning and promotes clear financial literacy, we help students confidently manage their finances, reduce stress, save time, and build a path toward a secure, debt-free future. Finally, we want to foster a holistic approach to financial wellness, helping students focus on their academics and extracurriculars without overworking.

KAREBARE

Our app, KareBare, is a virtual chat box designed to improve social media literacy and promote healthy online interactions for young users.

It features personalized chat interactions, screen time alerts, and provides resources for dealing with online harassment and maintaining a positive self-image. Through interactive conversations, Kare Bare aims to educate and assist young users in navigating the complexities of social media, empowering them to make informed decisions and engage safely online.

Design Problem

Young users need help learning social media literacy because knowing how to navigate online interactions is crucial for their personal development, but lack a proper support system to deal with online harms such as harassment, cyberbullying, and avoiding bad actorsß

Co-Ala (App Design Development 2024)

Improving communication between college students in long-distance relationships by allowing for stronger passive conveyance of emotions and providing a creative bonding outlet

Design Problem: College students in long-distance relationships struggle to communicate adequately with their partners due to their busy schedules. Text, as their main mode of communication, is ineffective at conveying intangibles like emotions and tone of voice, leading to misunderstandings that make navigating long-distance relationships even more difficult.

User Research: LDR Couples primarily communicate and connect at night Most LDR couples engage in some virtual activity to maintain the ‘spark’ in their relationship Misunderstandings and miscommunications often arise in LDRs due to text being the primary mode of communication and interaction. It's difficult to convey emotional nuances through text alone, especially if couples don’t speak frequently throughout the day.
